Notable Technical Projects

Here's what I've been working on recently in Engineering and AI!

Baemax, Health Robot

A college companion that sits on your desk and provides feedback on your health data, taken in through your smartphone or smart watch (currently compatible with iOS devices).

Kiwi, Socially Assistive Robot

A robot and tablet system with a reinforcement learning algorithm for selecting qualitatively oriented educational games geared towards the individual learning levels of students with Autism.

Smart Pantry, Break-to-Make Hackathon at USC

A system that can be installed in a pre-existing pantry which monitors items that are placed and removed on each shelf and stores the data in a mobile-friendly website and constructs a shopping list for each week's necessities.

Active Posture Adjusting Ergonomic Chair

A chair that tracks and corrects posture in real-time based on deviations from a straight-back sitting position (stored initially by user) using a matrix of ultrasonic sensors (for sensing) and linear servos (for adjusting).

Parkinson's Pen

A pen designed for Parkinson's patients that uses gyroscope technology to counteract hand tremors and produce smoother, more readable handwriting. This product was designed as an attachment to the back of a normal pen and can also be attached to other handheld tools.

Heart Rate from Video Feed

A program that can take in video feed of the face and return the heart rate of the individual during the video, based on subtle changes in color of the face as blood flows through one's veins. This has potential applications for military veterans, students with Autism, and patients with conditions that require monitoring stress levels.

Gender from Audio Files

A program that uses an intelligent system that was trained with machine learning to determine whether the speaker of an inputted audio file is male or female, and outputs the gender of the speaker.
